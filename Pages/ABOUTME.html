THE BEGINNINGS

In 2010, he approached international karting. He signed for the CRG team to race for their team in the world and European championships. In the KF3 World Cup, he finished second behind the experienced Alex Albonb he beat Albon in the WSK Euro Series. He also won the WSK World Series, beating Robert Vişoiu

In 2011, he won the WSK Euro Series on a CRG-powered grid. For 2012, he was signed by the Intrepid Driver Program to compete in the KF2 and KZ2 championships. He won the WSK Master Series in the KF2 class, beating CRG driver Felice Has. He won the South Garda Winter Cup in the KF2 class, beating Dennis Olsen and Antonio Fuoco.

In 2013, he won the KF and KZ European Championships. At the age of 15, he won the 2013 KZ world championship in Varennes-sur-Allier, France, in KZ1, the highest category in karting.

FORMULA 3 EUROPEAN

In 2014, he participated in the European Formula 3 with the Van Amersfoort Racing team. On May 4, he won the third race at Hockenheim from pole position after starting. In the second race he was on pole but had to drop out due to a problem with his gearbox. At Spa-Francorchamps, he became the first driver in 2014 to take three wins in one weekend. A week later at the Norisring he again won three races in a row. At the Nürburgring circuit he won his eighth race. Verstappen won ten races but was beaten by another class rookie, Esteban Ocon, who won nine races plus another nine second-place finishes. He eventually finished third in the championship

FORMULA 1

In August 2014, he joined the Red Bull Junior Team. Six days later, he was confirmed as one of the Scuderia Toro Rosso drivers for 2015. At seventeen years and one hundred and sixty-four days old, he would become the Youngest driver to start a World Championship race.

On Sunday, March 29, 2015, he became the youngest driver to score points in F1, at the Malaysian Grand Prix. Later, between reliability problems and some driver error, he could not score again until 5 racing afterwards. However, he bounces back in a big way at the Hungarian Grand Prix, where he took fourth place, a result he repeats in Austin.

F1 WORLD CHAMPION

The 2021 season was for many the best in the entire history of the Formula 1 category and of all motorsports, since after several years of overwhelming dominance by the driver Lewis Hamilton, Max Verstappen reached the last race of the season. equal in points with his rival Lewis, which was decided on the last lap with Max taking the victory and the eternal glory of being the Formula 1 world champion.
